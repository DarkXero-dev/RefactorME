Section On Everyday Life
Reading 6 minutes

I keep a notebook on the windowsill because that is where the light lands first. Most of what I write starts as a small observation: the woman who sells figs on the corner, the sound the tram makes on the hill, the particular way this city smells after rain.

These essays are not about anywhere important. They are about the machinery of an ordinary day, the parts we stop noticing once we live somewhere long enough. I try to notice again, and to write it down before it settles into habit.

I write slowly and I publish rarely. A piece leaves the flat only when it has earned the trip. If you are the kind of reader who prefers one good letter a month over a feed that never stops, I think you will feel at home here.

There is no schedule and no theme beyond attention. Cities, kitchens, strangers, and the long afternoons in between. Pull up a chair by the window; the coffee is still warm.
